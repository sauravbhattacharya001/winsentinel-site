WinSentinel vs Vanta: FAQ

Is WinSentinel an alternative to Vanta? +

They solve different halves of the same problem. Vanta is a compliance automation platform that collects evidence and tracks controls for SOC 2, HIPAA, ISO 27001 and similar audits across your SaaS and cloud. WinSentinel is a free Windows agent that actually finds and fixes the endpoint misconfigurations behind many of those controls, and maps every finding to CIS Windows L1, SOC 2 and HIPAA. Many teams run WinSentinel to harden the machines and a platform like Vanta to manage the audit paperwork.

What's the difference between Vanta and WinSentinel? +

Vanta monitors and reports whether a control is satisfied and gathers evidence for auditors; it does not remediate a misconfigured Windows machine for you. WinSentinel audits the Windows configuration directly with 33 native modules, gives a 0-100 posture score, and one-click fixes the findings. It is remediation-first on the endpoint rather than evidence-collection-first for the auditor.

How much does WinSentinel cost compared to Vanta? +

WinSentinel is free for unlimited use on a single machine. Vanta is a subscription compliance platform typically priced in the several thousands to tens of thousands of dollars per year depending on frameworks and company size (pricing is quote-based). WinSentinel Pro - which adds fleet-wide posture, remote fixes and cross-node compliance rollups across many machines - is $29/mo for up to 25 nodes or $79/mo for up to 100 nodes, with annual billing saving 17%.

Does WinSentinel help with SOC 2 or HIPAA? +

Yes. WinSentinel maps its Windows findings to CIS Windows L1, SOC 2 and HIPAA control areas so you can see which endpoint controls pass or fail and fix the failures. On a single machine this is free. WinSentinel Pro aggregates those findings across an entire fleet into organization-level compliance rollups you can export - complementary to an audit platform like Vanta rather than a replacement for it.

Is WinSentinel really free? +

Yes. The CLI and every audit module are free and open source under the MIT license, installed with dotnet tool install --global WinSentinel.Cli. A single machine gets the full power - all audit modules, the real-time monitor, scheduled scans, and PDF reports - with no limits and no account required. Pro is only for organizations that want to manage many machines from one control plane.
